The week AI video
lost Sora

OpenAI pulled the plug on Sora. Tencent open-sourced a Seedance-class video model overnight. And the entire ecosystem is recalibrating around a new generation of tools — HDR, multilingual audio, real-time voice control, and 15-second clips now standard across every major player.

Breaking

Industry-defining development

OpenAI shut down Sora

March 24, 2026. OpenAI discontinued Sora's consumer app and API, pivoting the research team toward "world simulation for robotics." The ~$1 billion Disney licensing deal — covering Marvel, Star Wars, and Pixar characters — was voided. Disney reportedly learned of the cancellation thirty minutes after an in-person meeting. Compute prioritization and strategic repositioning toward enterprise and AGI ahead of an IPO are cited. The most visible consumer AI video product is gone.

What this means: A significant competitive vacuum opens in consumer and independent filmmaker-facing AI video. The Disney partnership collapse signals ongoing regulatory and reputational risk for content-hungry AI video companies. Watch for studios and independents who built Sora workflows to migrate toward Veo 3.1, Kling 3.0, and Runway — or go open-source.

Source: CBS News · LA Times · Al Jazeera · VentureBeat

Most significant research development

OmniWeaving — open-source unified video gen

Tencent Hunyuan and Zhejiang University dropped OmniWeaving on arXiv March 25. It rivals proprietary Seedance-2.0 with free-form multimodal composition — text, multiple images, and video inputs combined. Its "thinking mode" generates reasoning-informed intermediate steps during generation. The architecture pairs an MLLM semantic parser with an MMDiT diffusion transformer and VAE. It introduces IntelligentVBench, the first benchmark for next-level unified video generation. Code and weights are under compliance review — public release expected in roughly one week.

Why it matters: If OmniWeaving ships cleanly with weights in hand, it becomes the go-to open alternative to Runway and Seedance for labs and indie shops alike. The "thinking mode" for video is a structural leap over current generation-as-dump approaches.

New Real-time Voice

Krea — Edit, Voice Mode, and a full redesign

Krea shipped three significant updates across the first two weeks of March. Krea Edit (March 9) adds region editing with brush and rectangle tools, object moving, border expansion, relighting with up to four virtual lights, camera angle simulation, and grayscale colorization. Voice Mode (March 2) enables real-time visual changes spoken aloud — a conversational loop between voice and visual output. The platform also got a full UI redesign. Krea aggregates Flux Krea, ChatGPT Image, Ideogram, Kling, and Minimax Hailuo behind a unified interface. Series B: $47M at $500M valuation (April 2025).

Multi-Model Quick Cut

Adobe Firefly — Quick Cut, 30+ models, Custom Model training

Adobe's March 19 expansion is the most comprehensive Firefly release to date. Quick Cut analyzes raw footage and generates a structured first cut from a text description. Firefly now routes across 30+ models including Google's Veo 3.1, OpenAI's GPT Image, Runway Gen-4.5, and Kling 2.5 Turbo. Luma Ray3 HDR is native inside Firefly. Google TurboQuant — 6× memory savings

Announced March 26: compression algorithm claiming ~6× memory savings on key-value cache for LLMs and vector search without retraining. Directly lowers inference costs for video generation infrastructure — worth tracking as it could meaningfully reduce cloud compute costs for AI filmmaking pipelines.

daVinci-MagiHuman — fully open-source 15B

A 15-billion parameter single-stream Transformer handling text, video, and audio via self-attention. Generates a 5-second 256p video in 2 seconds; 5-second 1080p in 38 seconds on a single H100. 80% win rate vs OVI 1.1, 60.9% vs LTX 2.3 in human evaluation. Fully open source — base, distilled, and super-resolution models with inference code.

LTX-2.3 — mature ComfyUI ecosystem

Lightricks' DiT-based audio-video foundation model. Distilled to 8 denoising steps with no CFG. Full ComfyUI support with active workflow collections. 1.13 million downloads and 761 likes on Hugging Face. One of the most pipeline-ready open video models currently available.

What Matters Most

Most strategically important

The Sora shutdown is the most significant competitive reconfiguring in AI video since the category existed. The vacuum doesn't automatically flow to any single player — Veo 3.1 and Kling 3.0 are the obvious technical beneficiaries, but neither has Sora's brand visibility with independent creators. The window to capture Sora's displaced user base is open now.

Most practically useful

OmniWeaving, if it releases cleanly. A fully open-source, multimodal, reasoning-aware video generation model with code and weights in hand would be immediately usable by any lab or indie team. The "thinking mode" generation is a genuine architectural novelty — watch for the public release in ~7 days.

Most infrastructure-relevant

Google TurboQuant and Intel's 32GB Arc Pro B70 GPU ($949) — both reduce the cost and hardware barrier for running capable diffusion models locally. For a startup building an AI filmmaking pipeline, this matters more than any single model release.
