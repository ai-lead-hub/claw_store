# Scoring Model

Use 1–5 scoring for important items.

## 1. Creative utility
How directly does this improve creative output?

- 1 = mostly irrelevant to filmmaking workflows
- 3 = somewhat useful or adjacent
- 5 = directly improves image/video/audio creation, editing, or control

## 2. Startup relevance
How much does this affect product strategy or market position?

- 1 = low strategic relevance
- 3 = useful context but not decisive
- 5 = meaningfully affects roadmap, positioning, or competition

## 3. Workflow leverage
How much time, cost, or complexity could it remove?

- 1 = minor convenience
- 3 = moderate improvement
- 5 = removes a major bottleneck or enables a new workflow

## 4. Proof level
How real is the signal?

- 1 = rumor or vague teaser
- 2 = announcement without evidence
- 3 = demo or early access proof
- 4 = shipped/beta with credible evidence
- 5 = shipped and already repeatable by users

## 5. Implementation nearness
How soon could this influence actual experiments or product decisions?

- 1 = long-term inspiration only
- 3 = plausible in the near future
- 5 = testable now

## 6. Monitor priority
How closely should we keep tracking it?

- 1 = low priority
- 3 = periodic watch
- 5 = high-priority ongoing watch

## Derived judgment

### High-priority item
Usually:
- strong creative utility and/or startup relevance
- decent proof level
- clear reason to summarize or promote

### Urgent item
Usually includes at least one of:
- major launch
- competitor move
- pricing shift
- workflow-changing release
- paper with unusually high implementation relevance

## Decision relevance
Also assign:
- `high`
- `medium`
- `low`

This is separate from hype. Ask:
"Does this help decide what to build, test, or ignore?"
